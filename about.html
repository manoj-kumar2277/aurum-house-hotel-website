Twenty-seven years of quiet hospitality

What began as a single restored townhouse in 1998 is now a forty-two suite house, rebuilt in 2021 without losing what made it worth visiting.

Our Story

Built on one idea: know the guest.

Aurum House opened in 1998 as a six-room guesthouse run by the Voss family out of a converted merchant's townhouse in the Old Quarter. The idea was simple — treat every guest the way you'd treat someone staying in your own home.

In 2021 the house was fully restored: original stonework preserved, mechanical systems replaced, and eighteen new suites added across two neighbouring buildings. The name changed to Aurum House, but the six original rooms — and the family's original notebook of guest preferences — are still in use today.
